The Work

What Transcription & Reporting Covers

From a single 30-minute interview to a series of recorded focus groups - accurate transcription with the option to go further and deliver it as a structured report.

Verbatim Transcription

Every word captured exactly as spoken, including fillers and false starts. Preferred for legal, HR, or research purposes where the exact wording matters.

Clean-Read Transcription

Edited for readability - fillers and repetitions removed, grammar lightly corrected. Ideal for publishing, sharing with clients, or inclusion in reports.

Multi-Speaker Labelling

Recordings with two or more speakers transcribed with each speaker's contributions clearly labelled - Interviewer / Respondent, or by name if provided.

Meeting Minutes

Recorded meetings turned into structured minutes - agenda items covered, decisions made, action items listed with owners and deadlines where mentioned.

Interview Reports

Qualitative interview transcripts turned into structured analytical reports - themes identified, key quotes extracted and findings summarised for research or HR use.

Lecture & Seminar Notes

Recorded academic lectures or seminars transcribed and formatted into clean, structured notes with headings, bullet points and key terms highlighted.

The Output

What You Get Back

A polished written document - ready to file, submit, publish, or pass to a colleague without any editing required on your end.

You Receive
  • Transcript or report in Word (.docx), PDF, or .txt
  • English transcriptions manually reviewed to native standard (IELTS C1)
  • Speaker labels and timestamps (on request)
  • Formatted to your preferred style or a clean default
  • Inaudible sections flagged honestly rather than guessed
  • Delivered via email or file-sharing link
How It Works
  • Send your audio or video file (MP3, MP4, WAV, etc.)
  • Confirm the format and level of detail you need
  • Standard turnaround: 24 hours per hour of audio
  • Rush delivery available - ask about availability
  • Revisions included for missed content or formatting
